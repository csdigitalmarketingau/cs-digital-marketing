If someone pulls out their phone right now and searches for what you do, does your business show up? Not buried on page three. Actually show up, in the map results, where people tap and call. That's what local SEO is about, and this guide will walk you through what matters.

## What Local SEO Actually Is

Local SEO is the process of getting your business to appear when people nearby search for your service. Not people in Sydney. People in Mandurah, Rockingham, Falcon, Halls Head, wherever you work.

When someone types "plumber Mandurah" or "hairdresser near me" into Google, a few things happen fast. Google looks at what businesses are nearby, how complete and trustworthy their information looks, and what other people have said about them. Then it decides who to show. Local SEO is how you make sure Google picks you.

It's different from general SEO, which is more about ranking a website nationally. Local SEO is about that map pack at the top of the results. The three listings with the star ratings and the phone numbers. That's the real estate worth chasing.

## Your Google Business Profile Is the Starting Point

If you haven't claimed your Google Business Profile, do that first. Everything else builds on it.

Your profile is what shows up in the map results. It's where your photos live, where your reviews sit, where people find your hours and your phone number. Google uses it to decide whether you're a real, active, relevant business.

A few things that matter here:

**Your business category.** Pick the most accurate primary category. If you're a carpenter in Mandurah, don't just put "contractor." Be specific.

**Your service area.** If you travel to Rockingham, Baldivis, or Pinjarra, tell Google that. It affects where you can show up.

**Photos.** Businesses with photos get more clicks. It doesn't need to be a professional shoot. Clear, real photos of your work, your premises, or your team are fine.

**Posts and updates.** Google lets you post updates directly to your profile. Most businesses never do it. That's actually an opportunity because Google notices activity.

Get the basics right on your profile and you're already ahead of a lot of businesses in Mandurah.

## Reviews Do More Work Than Most People Realise

Google reviews affect your ranking. They also affect whether someone calls you or your competitor.

More reviews, more recent reviews, and higher ratings all push you up. But it's not just about the number. Google also reads what people write. If your reviews mention "Mandurah," your service type, or specific jobs you've done, that signals relevance to Google.

The most common problem I see is businesses that have done great work for years but have almost no reviews online. Their competitor has 40 reviews and ranks above them, even though the competitor isn't better.

Asking for reviews feels awkward at first. It gets easier. After a job's done and the customer's happy, just ask. Most people are glad to help if they had a good experience. You can send them a direct link to your Google review page so there's no friction.

Don't ask for reviews in bulk all at once. A steady trickle over time looks more natural to Google than 20 reviews in a week.

## Your Website Still Matters

Your Google Business Profile and your website work together. Google looks at your website to understand what you do and where you do it.

A few things that help:

**Mention your location on your site.** If your site never says "Mandurah" or names the suburbs you service, Google has less reason to show you for local searches. A simple "serving Mandurah, Rockingham, and surrounds" in your footer or on your contact page makes a difference.

**A proper contact page.** Your address, phone number, and service area, clearly written out. Not hidden, not just in an image Google can't read.

**Speed on mobile.** Most local searches happen on phones. If your site loads slowly or looks broken on mobile, people leave fast. Google notices that too.

You don't need a massive site. A clean, fast, five-page website that clearly explains what you do and where you do it will outperform a bloated site that loads slowly.

## Consistency Across the Web

Your business name, address, and phone number need to match everywhere they appear online. Google Maps, your website, any directories you're listed in, Facebook, wherever.

If your address is listed differently in different places, that creates doubt for Google. It sounds minor but it does matter, especially if you've moved premises, changed your trading name, or updated your phone number at some point.

Have a look at what comes up when you search your own business name. Check that the details match. Fix anything that doesn't.
