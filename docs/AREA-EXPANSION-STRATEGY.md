# Area Expansion Strategy (Internal)

How CSDM expands its own visibility and client base beyond Mandurah, and how that shapes
outreach. Internal only. Pairs with the area pages (Mandurah / Rockingham / Perth) and the
GBP package playbook.

## The hard constraint
The **Google Map Pack is proximity-locked to your physical base.** CSDM's profile is pinned in
Mandurah/Falcon. No amount of optimisation makes a Mandurah pin rank in the *Perth* or
*Bunbury* map pack against businesses physically located there. Service-area settings signal
coverage but do not beat distance. (We confirmed this live: a 60-review, 20-year competitor in
Port Kennedy ranks *below* our new profile for "digital marketing Mandurah", purely because
we are closer and more relevant to Mandurah.)

So "rank in the map pack everywhere" is not a real plan for a single-location agency. Don't
chase it.

## The reframe
CSDM is a **remote agency**, so it does not need the map pack in every city. It needs **leads**.
Leads come from three things that are NOT distance-bound:
1. **Organic search** (results below the pack) - area pages can rank for "digital marketing
   Rockingham", "SEO Bunbury", "marketing agency Perth" regardless of where the pin is.
2. **Outreach** - cold calls and email are geo-agnostic.
3. **Proof that travels** - reviews and case studies (e.g. Gathered by Shanda hitting #1) sell
   in any area.

**Principle: own the map pack at home (Mandurah); win everywhere else through organic +
outreach + proof.**

## Per-area protocol
- **Mandurah (home base):** go for map pack top 3. Proximity works *for* us here. This is the
  ranking priority. Driven by reviews + activity + the optimisation already done.
- **Rockingham (adjacent):** a genuine map-pack shot in the **southern suburbs** (Baldivis,
  Singleton, Golden Bay) because they sit close to our Mandurah base. Central/north Rockingham
  is weaker on proximity. Back it with the Rockingham area page + a Rockingham client for local
  proof.
- **Bunbury (~70km, distant):** **organic only.** Build a Bunbury area page when there's a
  reason to. Do not chase the pack. Win clients via outreach + organic.
- **Perth (the giants):** do **not** fight the metro map pack head-on. Entrenched competitors
  plus proximity make it terrible ROI for a Mandurah solo operator. Win Perth via organic
  long-tail (suburb/niche pages), outreach, and case studies. The map pack there would require
  a genuine Perth location, which is not worth it now.

## Levers that travel (build over time)
Area and content pages, **backlinks and local citations**, reviews, **case studies**, and
outreach. These lift visibility everywhere, unlike the pack.

## The one hard rule
**No fake or duplicate GBP listings** for other cities (virtual offices, a "CSDM Perth" pin at
an address we don't occupy). That is the spam playbook Google suspends for, and it risks the
real listing too. One legitimate GBP per real location.

## When to expand
Let **client wins lead, not page-spinning.** Land a client in an area, turn it into a local
case study, and that makes both ranking and selling in that area easier. Demand and proof
first, then double down on that area's content.

## How this guides outreach (the key link)
Ranking and outreach are two halves of the same map:
- **Where we can rank (Mandurah, southern Rockingham):** the map pack + organic do a lot of the
  lead generation for us, so outreach there is a top-up, not the main engine.
- **Where we cannot rank the pack (Bunbury, Perth, north Rockingham):** **outreach is the
  primary way in.** Weight cold calls and email toward these areas, because we can't lean on the
  map pack to surface us. Organic area pages support the pitch ("you found us, and here's proof
  we rank others"), but the first contact is outreach.
- **Proof loop:** each client won in a new area becomes the case study that makes the *next*
  client in that area easier to win and easier to rank for. So early outreach into a new area is
  also an investment in future organic + sales traction there.

Net: Mandurah is the map-pack fortress; every other area is an organic-plus-outreach game, and
outreach should lean hardest into the areas we cannot rank our way into.
